## What "Budget" Means on the Kakobuy Spreadsheet

Budget does not mean "buy the cheapest row no matter what."

On the Kakobuy Spreadsheet, budget usually means:

- Basics under roughly **$50–$300**
- Simple silhouettes with lower QC risk
- Items where small flaws do not ruin the product
- Pieces that stay useful even if your first haul teaches you a lesson

Examples of strong budget categories:

- Plain tees
- Socks and caps
- Simple crossbody bags
- Basic AF1 / Dunk-style shoes
- Lightweight accessories
- Plain hoodies without heavy prints

The key insight from experienced buyers: **about 60% of a normal wardrobe haul can be budget-tier** if you know where cheap is smart and where cheap is false economy.

## Save vs Spend: The Real 2026 Rulebook

| Item | Budget option | Premium option | Verdict |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Plain white tee | Thin but fine | Heavier cotton | **Save** |
| Plain socks / cap | Usually good enough | Rarely worth upgrading | **Save** |
| Plain hoodie, no print | Basic fleece | Better cut and weight | **Split** |
| Printed tee / hoodie | Print may crack or shift | Better placement and ink | **Spend** |
| AF1 / simple Dunk | Shape is often close enough | Better leather and shape | **Save** on first pair |
| AJ4 / Travis / complex collab | Flaws show fast | Better materials and details | **Spend** |
| Puffer / shell jacket | Thinner, less accurate | Better insulation and fit | **Spend** if it is your main piece |
| Statement bag | Material can feel off | Better hardware and leather | **Spend** only if it is the centerpiece |
| Phone case / keychain | Budget is usually fine | Premium rarely matters | **Save** |

Budget shopping is not about avoiding premium forever. It is about **not wasting premium money on low-risk items**.

## The Budget Formula Most Beginners Miss

The product price is never the real price.

You need to think in **landed cost**:

```text
Item price
+ domestic shipping to warehouse
+ Kakobuy fees
+ international shipping
+ optional insurance
+ customs risk buffer
```

### Example mental model

If your cart is ¥760 in products, do not stop there. Ask:

- Will this parcel likely weigh 2 kg or 4 kg?
- Am I shipping one shoe pair or two?
- Did I add a jacket that doubles freight?

A ¥199 hoodie is not really ¥199 once shipping and weight are included.

## How to Keep Your First Haul Small Enough

These rules work well in practice:

### Rule 1: Start with 3–6 items

Not 12. Not 15. A small haul teaches:

- Cart flow
- Warehouse QC
- Freight calculation
- Sizing mistakes

### Rule 2: Keep one main item only

One hoodie **or** one shoe pair as the "main" piece. Not both plus a jacket plus two bags.

### Rule 3: Cap one expensive item at 30% of product budget

If your product budget is $100, do not let one item eat $60 unless it is the entire reason for the haul.

### Rule 4: Leave the cart for 24 hours

Budget finds look amazing at night. A next-day review removes a lot of bad decisions.

## Shipping Tricks That Matter More on Budget Hauls

Budget items only save money if freight does not erase the savings.

### Remove shoe boxes

This is one of the best budget-hauling tricks. Boxes add weight and volume for no product value.

### Ship together

Do not ship one tee alone. Build a small warehouse bundle first, then pay freight once.

### Use rehearsal / pre-pack when available

You want the real weight before choosing a line. Budget hauls die when freight surprises you at the end.

### Keep weight near 2–4 kg for a first parcel

Many buyers use a first haul around this range because it is easier to manage and less painful if something goes wrong.

## Budget QC: Where Beginners Still Lose Money

Budget does not mean "skip QC."

Budget items often have **higher flaw rates**, which means warehouse photos matter more, not less.

Before shipping, check:

- Print placement
- Logo accuracy
- Sole shape
- Tag and size label
- Zipper and stitching
- Color compared with seller photos

If a budget item looks wrong in QC, do not tell yourself "it was cheap anyway." Return or exchange before international shipping.

## Common Budget-Haul Mistakes

### 1. Buying hype on a budget batch

Travis Scott Jordans, complex AJ4s, and statement bags are where budget shopping fails hardest.

### 2. Adding too many shoes

Shoes are heavy. Two pairs can wreck a budget freight plan.

### 3. Mixing too many heavy items

Hoodie + jacket + boots + bag sounds fine in the cart and feels expensive at shipment.

### 4. Ignoring platform workflow

A cheap Weidian find still needs the correct Kakobuy order method. Budget price does not simplify that.

### 5. Treating "under $20" as free

Ten small items become a real shipping bill together.

## FAQ

### Is a budget first haul actually worth it?

Yes. It is the lowest-risk way to learn Kakobuy, QC, and freight without overspending on grails too early.

### What is the safest budget category?

Socks, caps, basic tees, and small accessories. If you want shoes, start with simple AF1 or Dunk-style listings.

### Should I ever pay more than budget on a first haul?

Yes — when the item is complex, printed, heavy, or meant to be the main piece of the haul.
