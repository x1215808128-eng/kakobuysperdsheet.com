Spreadsheet prices are easy to browse. **International shipping is where most first-time buyers get surprised.** You can build a smart cart on the **Kakobuy Spreadsheet**, pass QC, and still overspend on freight if you do not understand consolidation, volumetric weight, and line selection.

This guide walks through the full path from approved warehouse items to a shipped parcel — with practical rules that keep Kakobuy Spreadsheet hauls predictable in 2026.

## Spreadsheet Prices vs. Real Shipping Costs

The Kakobuy Spreadsheet on [KakobuyPlus](https://www.kakobuyplus.com/en/products) shows product-level USD pricing to help you compare finds. Those numbers **do not include**:

- Kakobuy service fees (often 0% on many orders — confirm at checkout)
- Domestic China shipping to warehouse
- International freight
- Fuel surcharges and remote area fees
- Optional services (rehearsal packaging, extra QC, vacuum seal)

Treat spreadsheet freight columns as **planning hints**, not invoices. Final quotes live inside your signed-in Kakobuy account after consolidation.

## Step 1: Approve QC Before You Think About Shipping

Never estimate a parcel while items still fail QC. Your final box composition changes if you:

- Reject and replace an item
- Exchange sizes
- Remove bulky shoeboxes

Complete QC review first — see our [QC Photos Guide](/kakobuy-spreadsheet-news/kakobuy-qc-photos-guide-2026) — then move to consolidation math.

## Step 2: Consolidate Multiple Spreadsheet Orders

Consolidation merges separate spreadsheet purchases into **one international shipment**.

| Without consolidation | With consolidation |
| --- | --- |
| 3 items × $25 shipping each | 1 parcel × ~$35–50 total |
| Multiple tracking numbers | Single tracking journey |
| Higher customs complexity | One declaration package |

### When to wait before shipping

- You expect more spreadsheet orders within 1–2 weeks
- You are building a themed haul (all shoes, seasonal wardrobe, etc.)
- You want to hit a weight tier that lowers per-kg cost

Kakobuy offers **180 days of free warehouse storage** on many accounts — use that window to batch intelligently instead of shipping impulse orders one by one.

### When to ship sooner

- Sale items with return windows closing
- Seasonal delivery deadlines (holidays, events)
- Items you need to test or resell quickly

## Step 3: Understand Chargeable Weight

Carriers bill on **chargeable weight** — the higher of:

- **Actual weight** on the scale
- **Volumetric weight** = (Length × Width × Height in cm) ÷ divisor

Divisors vary by lane (commonly 5000–8000 depending on line). Always read the divisor shown in Kakobuy's quote UI — not a spreadsheet formula copied from Discord.

### Why this matters for spreadsheet hauls

| Item type | Weight impact |
| --- | --- |
| Shoes in original boxes | High volumetric — boxes add dead air |
| Hoodies / tees | Soft goods — often actual weight wins |
| Bags with stuffing | Can inflate volume |
| Mixed hauls | Packing orientation changes everything |

**Pro tip:** Removing shoeboxes or using vacuum packing can drop volumetric weight dramatically — but only if you accept the packaging trade-off.

## Step 4: Choose the Right Shipping Line

| Line type | Cost | Speed | Best for |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| EMS | $$$ | 7–15 days | Balanced speed + tracking |
| SAL / economy post | $ | 15–40+ days | Budget, non-urgent hauls |
| DHL / FedEx express | $$$$ | 3–7 days | Time-critical deliveries |
| Sea freight | $ | 30–60 days | Large 10kg+ hauls |

### Selection rules

- **First haul under $150?** Prefer EMS or a mid-tier line with tracking — not the cheapest untracked option.
- **Heavy shoe haul?** Compare rehearsal packaging quotes before committing.
- **Mixed spreadsheet cart?** Estimate with Kakobuy's [shipping estimator](https://www.kakobuy.com/tools/estimate) using honest dimensions.

Add **7 days buffer** to every advertised ETA. Early delivery is a bonus; late delivery is normal during peak seasons.

## Step 5: Rehearsal Packaging (Optional but Valuable)

Rehearsal packaging simulates how Kakobuy will pack your parcel **before** you pay final freight. Use it when:

- Your cart mixes rigid shoe boxes and soft clothing
- Spreadsheet weight guesses differ from warehouse reality
- You are chasing a coupon weight breakpoint
- Freight quotes feel unexpectedly high

Rehearsal replaces guesswork with numbers tied to your actual SKU mix — especially helpful after QC rejections change your final item list.

## Step 6: Declarations and Customs Basics

When you ship:

- Declare contents accurately — vague or false declarations risk seizures
- Match declared value to a reasonable order total
- Keep screenshots of approved QC items for personal records
- Understand your country's import threshold and duty rules

Spreadsheet communities share declaration tips, but **your signed-in Kakobuy forwarding docs** override anecdotal advice.

## Cost-Saving Checklist for Spreadsheet Buyers

1. **Batch orders** — consolidate instead of one-item ships
2. **Review QC carefully** — avoid shipping items you would return locally
3. **Compare lines inside Kakobuy** — not spreadsheet comment columns
4. **Consider box removal** for personal-use shoe hauls
5. **Use rehearsal** when volumetric weight looks suspicious
6. **Plan around Chinese holidays** — warehouse and carrier delays stack
7. **Keep first haul modest ($50–$120)** — learn freight mechanics cheaply

## Common Shipping Mistakes

- Shipping immediately after the first QC approval while more orders are incoming
- Choosing fastest line without comparing cost per kg
- Trusting spreadsheet freight columns over Kakobuy's live quote
- Ignoring volumetric weight on shoe-heavy hauls
- Skipping rehearsal on mixed-density carts
- Declaring unrealistic values to "save customs" — high risk

## Bottom Line

The **Kakobuy Spreadsheet** helps you find products efficiently. **Shipping discipline** determines whether that efficiency survives the last mile. Consolidate with intention, respect chargeable weight math, pick lines that match your timeline and budget, and use Kakobuy's estimator and rehearsal tools when spreadsheet guesses stop matching reality.

Do that once on a small haul and every future spreadsheet cart becomes easier to plan — from link paste to doorstep delivery.
